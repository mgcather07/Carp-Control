About

Rough-Fish Removal, Real Adventure

Bowfishing is part archery, part hunt — you're on the water after dark, scanning the shallows under bright lights and taking aim at carp, gar, and other rough fish. It's fast, hands-on, and unlike anything you've done with a rod and reel.

Carp Control was built on a simple idea: invasive carp are hard on our waters, and thinning them out is a blast. Whether you're a seasoned archer or you've never held a bow, your guide handles the boat, the gear, and the know-how so you can focus on the shot.

  • Bows sized for adults and kids
  • Patient coaching for first-time shooters
  • Local waters and honey holes
  • Safe, well-equipped boat

Good to Know

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need any experience?

Not at all. Most of our shooters are first-timers. Your guide walks you through the gear and technique before you take your first shot.

What should I bring?

A valid fishing license, weather-appropriate clothes, closed-toe shoes, bug spray, and any snacks or drinks you'd like. We provide all bowfishing gear. (Confirm your local license requirements before your trip.)

What fish will we target?

Primarily common and grass carp, buffalo, and gar — the rough fish that are legal to take by bow in our area. Species and numbers vary by season and water conditions.

Is it safe for kids?

Yes. We have lighter bows for younger shooters and keep safety first all night. Reach out about age recommendations for your crew.

What happens to the fish?

Bowfishing targets invasive and rough fish that can't be legally released. We handle them responsibly — ask about cleaning and take-home options.

What if the weather is bad?

Safety comes first. If conditions won't cooperate, we'll work with you to reschedule for another night.
